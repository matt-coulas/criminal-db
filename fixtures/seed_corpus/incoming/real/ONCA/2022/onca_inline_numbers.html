Overview

[1] The appellant appeals his conviction for impaired driving causing bodily harm, contrary to s. 320.13(2) of the Criminal Code, R.S.C., 1985, c. C-46. He raises a single ground: whether the trial judge erred in admitting evidence of breath-analyser readings absent strict compliance with s. 320.31(1)(a).

[2] For the reasons that follow, we would dismiss the appeal.

Background

[3] On the evening of March 4, 2022, the appellant struck another vehicle on a residential street in the City of Toronto. The driver of the other vehicle sustained a fractured collarbone. The investigating officer formed grounds to demand a breath sample and the appellant was arrested at the scene.

[4] At the police station, two breath samples were taken using an Intoxilyzer 8000C. Both samples returned readings in excess of 160 mg of alcohol in 100 mL of blood. The appellant was charged accordingly.

The Trial Judge's Reasons

[5] The trial judge accepted the breath-technician's evidence as to the calibration of the device and concluded that the requirements of s. 320.31(1)(a) were satisfied notwithstanding a minor irregularity in the recorded ambient air check.

[6] He held that the irregularity was not such as to undermine the reliability of the readings. He further held that, even if there were a technical breach, the readings would be admissible under the residual common-law discretion.

Discussion

[7] We agree with the trial judge that strict compliance is not the standard. As this court explained in R. v. Goldson, the requirements of s. 320.31 are intended to safeguard the reliability of the readings, not to impose ritualistic procedural formalism on otherwise reliable evidence.

[8] The appellant relies on R. v. Lightfoot but that case is distinguishable. In Lightfoot the breath technician deviated from the manufacturer's protocol in a manner that materially affected the reliability of the readings. The present record discloses no such deviation.

Disposition

[9] The appeal is dismissed. The conviction and the sentence stand.
